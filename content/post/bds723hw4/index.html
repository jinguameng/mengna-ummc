Question 7

If an individual has alleles of A and a in a locus, what types of gamete can be made? and what is the probability for each type of gamete? (Score: 5)

Two possiable gametes: A and a.

Pr(A) = 0.5 and Pr(a) = 0.5

Question 8

If an individual has alleles of A and a at a locus of chr 1, but only allele B at a locus of chr 2, what types of gamete can be made? and what is the probability for each type of gamete? (Score: 5)

Two possiable gametes: AB and aB.

Pr(AB) = 0.5 and Pr(aB) = 0.5

Question 9

If an individual has alleles of A and a at a locus of chr 1, and alleles of B and b at a locus of chr 2, what types of gamete can be made? and what is the probability for each type of gamete? (Score: 5)

Four possiable gametes: AB, aB, Ab, ab.

Pr(AB) = Pr(aB) = Pr(Ab) = Pr(ab) = 0.25

Question 11
